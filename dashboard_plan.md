# Dashboard Design Plan

## 1) Executive Dashboard
- KPI Cards: Total Revenue, Total Profit, Profit Margin, Total Orders, AOV.
- Visuals: Monthly revenue line chart, revenue by category bar chart, region map.
- Filters/Slicers: Date range, Region, Segment, Category.
- Why it matters: Gives leadership a one-page business pulse.

## 2) Sales Dashboard
- KPI Cards: Monthly Revenue, YoY Growth, Discount Rate, Avg Units per Order.
- Visuals: Revenue trend (line), top 10 products (bar), discount vs profit (scatter).
- Filters/Slicers: Category, Sub-Category, Ship Mode, Month.
- Why it matters: Helps sales teams track performance drivers quickly.

## 3) Customer Dashboard
- KPI Cards: Active Customers, New Customers, Repeat Purchase %, Avg Customer Revenue.
- Visuals: Top customers table, segment revenue split (donut), customer growth trend.
- Filters/Slicers: Segment, Region, Date range.
- Why it matters: Supports retention and account prioritization.

## 4) Product Dashboard
- KPI Cards: Top Product Revenue, Top Product Profit, Low-profit Product Count.
- Visuals: Product revenue treemap, sub-category margin bar chart, bottom products table.
- Filters/Slicers: Category, Sub-Category, Region, Discount band.
- Why it matters: Enables assortment and pricing decisions.

## Visual Best Practices
- Use a consistent color system: Revenue = blue, Profit = green, Loss = red.
- Keep KPI cards at top, trends in middle, detailed tables at bottom.
- Limit charts per page to 6-8 for readability.
- Add dynamic titles (e.g., "Revenue Trend - East Region").
- Include tooltips with revenue, profit, and margin for context.
